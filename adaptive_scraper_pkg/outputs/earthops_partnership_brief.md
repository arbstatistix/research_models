# EarthOps Partnership Brief
## Business Meeting Preparation Document

*Prepared for: We Foundation Leadership Team*
*Subject: Strategic Partnership Exploration with EarthOps*
*Date: April 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

This document provides a strategic analysis of EarthOps as a potential partnership target for We Foundation. EarthOps operates a B2B vertical social platform designed as the "operating system" for environmental sustainability collaboration. The platform's architecture—combining social networking, knowledge management, marketplace functionality, and gamification—presents multiple integration points for We Foundation's core programs: river and environmental cleanup, chula (biomass cookstove) distribution, robotics education, school transformation, and student sustainability engagement.

---

## About EarthOps

### Corporate Structure

EarthOps is a vertical spinoff of **PeopleMovers Inc.**, a Detroit-based technology company founded by **Keith Zendler** (CEO). The parent company develops community development platforms; EarthOps applies this technology specifically to environmental sustainability.

- **Founded:** 2023
- **Headquarters:** Detroit, Michigan, USA
- **Funding Status:** Bootstrapped (self-funded)
- **India Presence:** Community Engagement lead — Aloysius John

### Platform Capabilities

EarthOps positions itself as the "LinkedIn for environmental sustainability," offering:

| Capability | Function |
|------------|----------|
| **Social Networking** | Professional profiles, organization pages, activity feeds, global directory |
| **Collaboration Hub** | Public/private groups, document sharing, event coordination, messaging |
| **Innovation Hub** | Repository of procedures and best practices; "GitHub for SOPs" |
| **Marketplace** | B2B service listings (recycling, waste management, equipment, consulting) |
| **EarthPerks®** | Gamification layer—points for engagement, redeemable rewards, custom programs |
| **Analytics** | Progress tracking, outcome measurement, stakeholder reporting |

### Target Customers

EarthOps targets manufacturing, governments, industry associations, foundations, healthcare/education facilities, construction, and environmental service providers.

## Strategic Partnership Opportunities

### 1. River & Environmental Cleanup Programs

**Alignment:** EarthOps serves municipal governments and industry associations coordinating multi-stakeholder environmental initiatives.

**Opportunity:**
- We Foundation's river cleanup methodology could be uploaded to the Innovation Hub as a replicable "procedure" for global adoption
- Cleanup events could be listed on EarthOps' Events module, attracting corporate volunteers and CSR participation
- Municipalities using EarthOps could discover and request We Foundation's services via the Marketplace

**Value Proposition to EarthOps:** Demonstrated impact content from India—a key emerging market where they have established presence through Aloysius John.

---

### 2. Chula (Biomass Cookstove) Distribution Program

**Alignment:** EarthOps' platform serves organizations pursuing emissions reduction, renewable energy, and supply chain sustainability.

**Opportunity:**
- The chula program addresses **Scope 3 emissions** for corporations with India supply chains (factory workers, agricultural communities)
- Carbon credit generation from LPG displacement could be tracked and verified through EarthOps' analytics
- Distribution partners (rural cooperatives, women's SHGs) could coordinate via EarthOps Groups
- The program qualifies for listing in EarthOps' Energy & Emissions marketplace category

**Value Proposition to EarthOps:** A tangible, measurable climate intervention that corporations can fund for ESG reporting—demonstrating platform utility beyond software.

---

### 3. Robotics Education in Schools

**Alignment:** EarthOps targets educational institutions for facility management and sustainability compliance. However, a gap exists in **student engagement** programming.

**Opportunity:**
- Position robotics education as "sustainability robotics"—environmental monitoring sensors, waste-sorting robots, water quality automation
- Student projects could generate Innovation Hub content (youth-developed SOPs, maker projects)
- EarthPerks® could gamify student participation, with rewards sponsored by corporate members
- Creates pipeline for future environmental professionals entering the EarthOps ecosystem

**Value Proposition to EarthOps:** Youth demographic engagement that no competitor offers; long-term user base development.

---

### 4. School & College Transformation

**Alignment:** Educational institutions represent a defined customer segment for EarthOps.

**Opportunity:**
- We Foundation could serve as an implementation partner for schools adopting EarthOps
- Campus sustainability audits, waste management programs, and green certification could be delivered through the platform
- Institutional transformation case studies would populate EarthOps' India success stories

**Value Proposition to EarthOps:** Ground-level implementation capability they currently lack; boots on the ground in Indian educational institutions.

---

### 5. Student Sustainability Projects & Events

**Alignment:** EarthOps' Events and Groups modules are designed for exactly this purpose.

**Opportunity:**
- We Foundation becomes the organizer of record for student sustainability competitions, hackathons, and innovation challenges
- EarthPerks® gamification incentivizes participation across institutions
- Winning projects enter the Innovation Hub as replicable models
- Corporate sponsors gain visibility and CSR engagement through platform integration

**Value Proposition to EarthOps:** Active content generation and engagement metrics that demonstrate platform vitality to enterprise customers.

## Proposed Partnership Models

### Model A: Service Provider Listing

We Foundation registers on EarthOps Marketplace as an environmental service provider, offering:
- River/lake cleanup consulting
- Chula distribution and carbon credit programs
- Educational sustainability programs
- Event management for sustainability initiatives

*Barrier to Entry: Low | Revenue Model: Fee-for-service*

---

### Model B: Content Partnership

We Foundation contributes to the Innovation Hub:
- Cleanup methodology SOPs
- Chula distribution playbooks
- School sustainability audit frameworks
- Student engagement program templates

In exchange, We Foundation receives:
- Platform visibility and branding
- Lead generation from organizations adopting these procedures
- Potential revenue share on consulting engagements

*Barrier to Entry: Medium | Revenue Model: Lead generation + consulting*

---

### Model C: India Implementation Partner

We Foundation becomes EarthOps' on-ground partner for the India market, responsible for:
- Onboarding educational institutions
- Coordinating government/municipal engagements
- Managing EarthPerks® reward fulfillment locally
- Serving as the face of EarthOps in sustainability events

In exchange:
- Revenue share on India subscriptions/transactions
- Co-branding and marketing support
- Priority access to platform features and corporate connections

*Barrier to Entry: High | Revenue Model: Revenue share + consulting*

---

### Model D: Custom EarthPerks® Program

Develop a white-labeled EarthPerks® program specifically for:
- Students participating in We Foundation programs
- Communities adopting chulas
- Volunteers in cleanup drives

This would extend gamification beyond the EarthOps platform to We Foundation's independent programs while maintaining data connectivity for impact reporting.

*Barrier to Entry: Medium-High | Revenue Model: Program fees + sponsorships*

## Key Discussion Points for Meeting

1. **India Market Strategy:** What are EarthOps' expansion plans for India? How does Aloysius John's role fit into this?

2. **Educational Vertical:** Is EarthOps actively pursuing schools and colleges? What gaps exist in their implementation capability?

3. **Gamification for Youth:** Has EarthOps considered student-focused EarthPerks® programs? What would sponsorship look like?

4. **Carbon Credit Integration:** Can the platform track and verify carbon credits from interventions like chula distribution?

5. **Marketplace Economics:** What are the fee structures for service providers? Lead generation vs. transaction fees?

6. **Technology Roadmap:** Given the AngularJS stack, what modernization is planned? Mobile-first considerations for India?

7. **Funding Trajectory:** With bootstrapped status, is EarthOps seeking strategic investors or partners with capital?

## Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Early-stage platform** (2023 founded, unfunded) | Start with low-commitment Model A; scale based on traction |
| **Unproven India presence** | Leverage existing Aloysius John relationship; propose pilot programs |
| **Tech stack concerns** (AngularJS is legacy) | Focus on content/services, not technical integration initially |
| **Platform dependency** | Maintain independent programs; use EarthOps as channel, not infrastructure |
| **Unclear revenue model** | Clarify fee structures before committing to exclusive arrangements |

## Recommended Approach

**Phase 1 (Immediate):**
- Register We Foundation as a service provider on EarthOps Marketplace
- Connect with Aloysius John to understand India operations
- Upload 2-3 procedures to Innovation Hub as proof of concept

**Phase 2 (3-6 months):**
- Evaluate platform engagement and lead quality
- Propose pilot EarthPerks® program for one school network
- Co-host one sustainability event through EarthOps Events

**Phase 3 (6-12 months):**
- Negotiate formal India partnership if Phase 1-2 demonstrate value
- Develop custom EarthPerks® program for chula adopters
- Position for deeper integration as platform matures
