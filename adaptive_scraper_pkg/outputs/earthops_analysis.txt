# EarthOps — Deep Dive Analysis

## The Parent Company: PeopleMovers Inc.

EarthOps isn't a standalone startup — it's a **vertical-specific spinoff** of **PeopleMovers**, a Detroit-based technology company founded by **Keith Zendler** (CEO). PeopleMovers is a broader "community development" platform, and EarthOps applies the same underlying technology to environmental sustainability.

**Founded:** 2023
**Headquarters:** Detroit, Michigan
**Funding Status:** Unfunded (bootstrapped/self-funded per Tracxn)

---

## The Core Problem They Solve

Environmental leaders (corporate sustainability officers, government officials, NGO directors) are **overwhelmed** by:
- Fragmented environmental management tools
- Disconnected reporting to shareholders, customers, and regulators
- Siloed best practices — every organization reinventing the wheel
- Difficulty training employees and vendors across distributed operations
- No single place to coordinate supply chain sustainability

**EarthOps' pitch:** One platform to "connect and manage all the dots of the environmental sustainability supply chain globally."

---

## The Platform: Feature Breakdown

### 1. **Social Networking Layer**
- Individual profiles for environmental professionals
- Organization pages (companies, governments, NGOs)
- Follow/following model like LinkedIn
- Activity feeds showing what leaders/organizations are doing
- Global directory of environmental stakeholders

### 2. **Collaboration Hub**
- **Groups:** Public or private workspaces for initiatives, teams, committees
- **News & Info:** Press releases, blogs, document sharing
- **Events:** Conferences, trainings, outreach, fundraisers
- **Private Messaging:** Direct communication between members

### 3. **Knowledge Management (The "Innovation Hub")**
- **Procedures:** Upload SOPs and best practices
- **Actions:** Assign tasks to employees, vendors, member organizations with tracking
- **Innovation Hub:** Copy best practices from other entities worldwide
- This is essentially a **GitHub for environmental procedures** — fork, adapt, deploy

### 4. **Marketplace (B2B Services)**
From the scraped data, they have extensive categories:
- Recycling markets (scrap metal, cardboard, plastics, glass)
- Waste management (hazardous, non-hazardous, medical, e-waste)
- Equipment (balers, compactors)
- Services (document shredding, transportation, warehousing)
- Energy & emissions (renewable energy, GHG management)
- Water management
- Training & consulting

This is a **two-sided marketplace**: Service providers list offerings; organizations request quotes.

### 5. **Gamification: EarthPerks®**
- Users earn points for engagement (posting, collaborating, completing actions)
- Points redeemable for food, beverages, tickets, eco-products
- Organizations can sponsor rewards to incentivize specific behaviors
- **SaaS upsell:** Custom EarthPerks programs for initiatives outside the platform

### 6. **Data & Analytics**
- Reports on activities across stakeholders
- Progress tracking on environmental initiatives
- Outcome measurement for foundations/grantmakers

## Strategic Positioning

**The Vision:** Become the **"LinkedIn for environmental sustainability"** — the default professional network where environmental leaders connect, collaborate, and transact.

**The Bet:** Environmental sustainability is becoming a core business function (ESG reporting, supply chain pressure, regulatory compliance). Organizations need better tools to coordinate these efforts across complex stakeholder networks.

**The Risk:**
- Platform chicken-and-egg problem (need users to attract users)
- Competing against established enterprise tools (Salesforce, SAP)
- Older tech stack may limit scalability

---

## Summary

EarthOps is a **B2B vertical social platform** attempting to own the "operating system" for environmental sustainability collaboration. It's not just a directory or a marketplace — it's trying to be the **coordination layer** for how organizations plan, execute, and measure environmental initiatives together. Think of it as: *LinkedIn + Notion + Upwork + ClassDojo* — but specifically for sustainability professionals.

The connection to PeopleMovers suggests this is part of a **platform strategy** where the same underlying technology gets white-labeled for different verticals (community development → environmental → potentially others).
